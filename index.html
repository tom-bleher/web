1.3.1 Simulation Studies for Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) Detector Design

Along with my studies, I work on simulation-based studies of AC-LGAD (RSD) detectors for the EIC. The goal is to better understand the constraints and requirements for building the Far-Backward (FB) and Far-Forward (FF) for the anticipated particle accelerator.

1.3.2 Construction of Impurity Transport Database, Development of Data Analysis Tools, and Profile Charactrization for Wendelstein 7-X

Fulfilling my dream of working in a nuclear fusion reactor, I was fortunate to take in Wendelstein 7-X, the world's leading Stellarator research facility. My research focused on the construction of a framework to study impurity transport under neutral beam injection (NBI). The motivation behind this is the understanding of impurity transport for reduction of radiative losses in future fusion powerplants.


The talk I gave for my work can be found in Talks.

X-Ray Flux Optimization by Correction of Optical Components

Motivated by the application of x-ray imaging to medial uses, I programmed optimization codes to optimize x-ray flux from LWFA by real-time correction of optical components.

Modeling and Planning of Direct Laser Acceleration (DLA) Deuteron Acceleration from Jet Targets

I assisted preperation for upcoming collaborative experiment with Stanford by modeling experimental setup using CAD software.
